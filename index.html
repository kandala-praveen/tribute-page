SIR AUTHUR THOMAS COTTON

“The man who turned rivers into lifelines for millions.”
“He is famous because he gave water to dry lands, saved people from famines, and made South India's deltas rich with agriculture.”

Full name: General Sir Arthur Thomas Cotton

Born:15 May 1803.

Died:24 July 1899.

Profession:British army officer turned irrigation & civil engineer.

“Timeline of Works”

1821 - Joined Madras Engineers as an officer in the East India Company.

1828-1836 - Worked on Cauvery Delta (Upper & Lower Anaicut works, embankments, canals) in Tamil Nadu.

1847-1852 - Constructed the Dowleswaram Barrage across the Godavari River, irrigating lakhs of acres in Andhra Pradesh.

1852-1855 - Extended irrigation canals and navigation channels in Godavari delta.

1854-1855 - Took up the Krishna River Anicut project (later known as Prakasam Barrage), improving irrigation in Krishna delta.

1860s - Proposed a grand plan for linking major rivers of India for navigation and famine prevention (interlinking rivers concept).

1862 - Retired from active service but continued writing and advising on irrigation projects.

1899 - Passed away in London at the age of 96.
